Add a top “Fantasy Outlook Summary” block

This is the single most important improvement.

What it should do

A 200–350 word paragraph block right near the top that explains:

what kind of fantasy asset the player is

how they scored last year

what drives their production

whether the trend is improving or declining

how managers should view them in redraft and dynasty

Example for Josh Allen

Something like:

Josh Allen enters 2026 as one of fantasy football’s highest-upside quarterbacks thanks to his dual-threat role and weekly touchdown ceiling. In 2025, Allen averaged 22.0 fantasy points per game and finished as a QB1 in 65% of his games, keeping him in the elite fantasy tier despite some late-season cooling.

His profile continues to be supported by rushing usage, which helps stabilize his weekly floor even when passing efficiency dips. Recent trend data showed an increase in rushing attempts, although overall production softened toward the end of the season. That combination still makes Allen one of the safest high-end quarterback options in both redraft and dynasty formats.

For fantasy managers, Allen’s value comes from his ability to produce spike weeks through both the air and on the ground. His risk is lower than most quarterbacks because his rushing role protects him from complete bust weeks, but some week-to-week volatility remains when touchdown production falls.

That kind of block is gold for SEO.
